# ATLAS Physics Adventure

The general objective of this capstone project is to develop “ATLAS”, a 2D edutainment mobile game
designed as a supplementary learning tool for Grade 12 STEM students at STI College Baliuag.
This game aims to enhance learning, understanding, and visualization of
General Physics 1 concepts through interactive simulations and engaging gameplay mechanics.

Featuring story-driven dialogues, bite-sized lessons, and interactive simulation and controls,
ATLAS leverages visual learning to present physics in a more dynamic, intuitive, and innovative way,
making complex concepts easier to grasp. By integrating interactive visualizations,
this game transforms the learning experience, making physics more fun, engaging, and effective.

## Features
- Main Menu – The starting point of the game, where players access all major features of the game.
- Campaign Mode – The story-driven gameplay experience where players progress through chapters and levels. It covers topics from General Physics 1 in curriculum order.
    - Chapters – The main story arcs, where players unlock and complete levels in a sequence. Each chapter may contain 5-8 levels.
    - Levels – Individual levels within a chapter, each designed with unique gameplay objectives to teach and assess physics concepts in engaging ways. Each level falls under one of the following types:
        - Pre-Test – Introduces the chapter storyline and tests the player's current knowledge of the topic with a short quiz. Each chapter begins with one pre-test.
        - Lesson – Bite-sized, interactive lessons that explain physics concepts, followed by a short quiz or problem-solving activity. Each chapter typically contains 1–3 lesson levels.
        - Simulation – Complete objectives through interactive physics simulations, experiments, and tool usage to explore and apply physics concepts. There will be 1-2 simulation levels per chapter.
        - Challenge – Special tasks requiring players to complete objectives within a time or attempt limit. Challenge levels primarily feature minigames designed to test the player’s understanding and application of physics concepts in a fun and engaging way. Each challenge is structured to reinforce the lessons from previous levels through interactive gameplay rather than quizzes or problem-solving activities. Each chapter includes one challenge level that serves as a culmination of the player’s learning progress.
      - Post-Test – A long quiz and math solving test that measures the player's mastery of the chapter’s topics and closes the chapter storyline. Each chapter ends with a post-test.
- Free Play Mode – Allow players to select quiz and solving topics, simulations, or minigames that have been unlocked through campaign mode, enabling gameplay without the storyline. The available game types include:
    - Quiz – Answer a variety of question formats, such as multiple choice, true or false, matching, fill in the blanks, sequencing, and categorization. The quiz system also includes problem-solving and computation-based questions, allowing players to read and solve math and equation problems directly within the game using the built-in scientific calculator.
    - Simulation – Complete objectives by interactive physics simulations, solving puzzles, navigating objects, testing graphs, or using virtual tools.
    - Minigame – Play short, casual games such as 2D platformers, top-down games, racing challenges, or simple interactive activities.
- Library – Revisit, review, and know more about certain physics topics, as well as access some tools.
    - Guidebooks – Collection of guidebooks containing summaries and detailed explanations of physics topics, unlocked as you complete lesson levels.
    - Glossary – Comprehensive list of physics concepts and terms providing definitions and context about it, unlocked as you encounter in lesson levels.
  - Scientific Calculator – Access built-in calculator to perform computations.
- Profile – Manage avatar, account and cloud save, as well as access game progress, achievements, statistics, and records.
    - Avatar – Edit game information such as display name, icon, age, gender, as well as select character and primary and secondary colors.
    - Account – Allow players to register, login, edit accounts, and manage cloud save data.
    - Progress – View your game progress including your campaign progress consisting of completed chapters, levels and obtained medals, and unlocked guidebooks and glossary terms.
    - Performance – View records such as pre-test and post-test scores per chapter, and best level scores and times, and statistics such as time spent playing, number of completes and fails, and more.
- Leaderboards – Display player rankings for campaign score and best level scores to see how you compare with other players and challenge yourself to push better.
- Settings – Customize game experience by adjusting master volume, music, UI, and game sound effects.
- Manual – View the complete game manual and instructions for detailed overview of gameplay mechanics, controls, and features.
- About – Learn more about the game, including its current version, update logs, development team, and credits for the contributors and resources that made the game possible.
